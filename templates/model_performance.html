5-Fold Stratified Cross-Validation

The dataset is split into 5 parts. The model is trained on 4 and tested on 1, repeated 5 times. This gives an honest estimate of performance on unseen data.

Verdict: CV accuracy of 83.66% ± 0.43% is stable across all folds (max spread: 1.25pp). This confirms the model generalises well — it's not just memorising training data.

Class Distribution (Imbalance)

The dataset is imbalanced — "High" is only 9.4% of samples. This is why Macro F1 (which treats each class equally) is more meaningful than raw accuracy here.

How to Interpret These Metrics

Accuracy — % of all predictions that are correct. Easy to game on imbalanced data.

Precision — Of students predicted as "High", what % actually were? Measures false positive rate.

Recall — Of all actual "High" students, what % did the model catch? Measures false negative rate.

F1 Score — Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Best single metric for imbalanced classes.

Macro F1 — Average F1 across all classes, giving equal weight to each. Best for imbalanced datasets.

ROC-AUC — Probability that the model ranks a positive case higher than a negative one. 1.0 = perfect, 0.5 = random.

Cross-Validation — Training and testing on different subsets to measure real generalisation, not just memorisation.
